# D&D 5e Combat Rules Reference (2014 + 2024 Updates)

## Turn Structure
1. Roll Initiative (d20 + Dexterity modifier) - determines turn order
2. Each turn: Move (up to speed) + 1 Action + possibly 1 Bonus Action + possibly 1 Reaction
3. Free interaction: speak briefly, interact with one object
4. After everyone goes, next round starts (same initiative order)

## Initiative
- d20 + Dexterity modifier
- Ties: players choose order among themselves, DM orders monsters
- 2024 UPDATE: Surprise = Disadvantage on Initiative roll (not lost turn)

## Actions in Combat
- **Attack**: Make melee or ranged attack(s). Extra Attack feature grants more.
- **Cast a Spell**: Most spells use 1 action. 2024: Only 1 leveled spell per turn.
- **Dash**: Gain extra movement equal to your speed.
- **Disengage**: Movement doesn't provoke opportunity attacks this turn.
- **Dodge**: Attacks against you have disadvantage, advantage on Dex saves.
- **Help**: Give advantage on ally's next ability check or attack roll.
- **Hide**: Make Stealth check. 2024: Set DC instead of vs Perception.
- **Ready**: Set trigger + reaction to act later. Uses reaction when triggered.
- **Search**: Perception or Investigation check.
- **Use an Object**: Use item requiring an action.

## Bonus Actions
- Only available when a feature/spell grants one
- Only 1 per turn
- Cannot be used as an Action (and vice versa)
- 2024: Potions can be used as Bonus Action

## Reactions
- 1 per round, resets at start of your next turn
- Triggered by specific events (can be on ANY creature's turn)
- **Opportunity Attack**: When creature you can see leaves your reach
  - 2024: Triggers on ANY creature (not just hostile), using action/bonus/reaction/speed
  - Make ONE melee attack (no Extra Attack)
  - Does NOT trigger from: teleportation, forced movement, or unseen creatures
- **Ready action**: Execute your readied action/spell on trigger
- **Reaction spells**: Shield, Counterspell, etc. (specific triggers)
- **Dismount reaction**: When mount knocked prone, land on feet

## Movement
- Move up to your speed on your turn
- Can break up movement (move, attack, move more)
- Can move between multiple attacks
- **Difficult terrain**: Costs 2x movement per foot
- **Prone**: Standing costs half your speed. Crawling = 2x cost.
- **Through allies**: Free movement through non-hostile creatures
- **Through enemies**: Only if 2+ sizes larger/smaller. Can't end in their space.
- **Flying**: Fall if knocked prone, speed reduced to 0, or deprived of movement

## Attack Resolution
1. Choose target in range
2. Determine modifiers (advantage/disadvantage, cover, spells)
3. Roll d20 + ability modifier + proficiency bonus
4. Compare to target's AC (Armor Class)
5. Meet or exceed = HIT
- **Critical Hit**: Natural 20 = auto-hit, double all damage dice
- **Critical Miss**: Natural 1 = auto-miss
- **Advantage**: Roll 2d20, take higher
- **Disadvantage**: Roll 2d20, take lower

## Melee Attacks
- Default reach: 5 feet
- Ability modifier: Strength (or Dex for finesse weapons)
- **Two-Weapon Fighting**: Bonus action attack with light off-hand weapon, no ability mod to damage (unless negative)
- **Grapple**: 2024: Unarmed strike variant, target makes saving throw (not contested check)
- **Shove**: 2024: Unarmed strike variant, knock prone or push 5ft, saving throw

## Ranged Attacks
- Two ranges: normal/long. Disadvantage beyond normal range.
- Can't target beyond long range.
- Disadvantage if hostile creature within 5 feet.
- Ability modifier: Dexterity

## Cover
- **Half cover**: +2 AC and Dex saves
- **Three-quarters cover**: +5 AC and Dex saves
- **Total cover**: Can't be targeted directly

## Damage
- Roll weapon/spell damage dice + ability modifier
- **Critical hit**: Double all damage dice (not modifiers)
- 13 damage types: Acid, Bludgeoning, Cold, Fire, Force, Lightning, Necrotic, Piercing, Poison, Psychic, Radiant, Slashing, Thunder
- **Resistance**: Half damage of that type
- **Vulnerability**: Double damage of that type
- **Immunity**: No damage of that type

## Dropping to 0 HP
- Fall unconscious (unless instant death)
- **Instant Death**: Remaining damage >= max HP = dead
- **Death Saving Throws**: Start of each turn at 0 HP
  - d20: 10+ = success, 9 or below = failure
  - 3 successes = stabilize (unconscious but stable)
  - 3 failures = dead
  - Natural 1 = 2 failures
  - Natural 20 = regain 1 HP, wake up
  - Taking damage at 0 HP = 1 failure (crit = 2 failures)
- **Stabilizing**: DC 10 Medicine check (action)
- **Knocking Out**: Melee attacker can choose to KO instead of kill at 0 HP

## Temporary Hit Points
- Buffer before real HP. Don't stack (choose higher).
- Can't be healed. Don't restore consciousness.

## Healing
- Can't exceed max HP
- Dead creatures can't be healed (need revivify etc.)

## Conditions
- **Blinded**: Can't see, auto-fail sight checks, attacks at disadvantage, attacks against have advantage
- **Charmed**: Can't attack charmer, charmer has advantage on social checks
- **Deafened**: Can't hear, auto-fail hearing checks
- **Exhaustion**: 2024: -2 per level to d20 rolls, -5ft speed per level, death at level 6
- **Frightened**: Disadvantage on checks/attacks while source visible, can't approach source
- **Grappled**: Speed = 0, ends if grappler incapacitated
- **Incapacitated**: Can't take actions or reactions
- **Invisible**: Can't be seen, attacks at advantage, attacks against at disadvantage
- **Paralyzed**: Incapacitated, can't move/speak, auto-fail Str/Dex saves, attacks have advantage, hits within 5ft are crits
- **Petrified**: Turned to stone, incapacitated, resistance to all damage, immune to poison/disease
- **Poisoned**: Disadvantage on attacks and ability checks
- **Prone**: Disadvantage on attacks, attacks within 5ft have advantage, attacks beyond 5ft have disadvantage
- **Restrained**: Speed 0, attacks at disadvantage, attacks against at advantage, disadvantage on Dex saves
- **Stunned**: Incapacitated, can't move, faltering speech, auto-fail Str/Dex saves, attacks have advantage
- **Unconscious**: Incapacitated, drops items, falls prone, auto-fail Str/Dex saves, attacks have advantage, hits within 5ft are crits

## 2024 NEW: Weapon Mastery
Available to: Barbarian, Fighter, Paladin, Ranger, Rogue (+ Weapon Master feat)
Each weapon has a mastery property:
- **Cleave**: On hit, attack another creature within 5ft (no ability mod to damage)
- **Graze**: On miss, deal ability modifier damage anyway
- **Nick**: Extra off-hand attack is part of Attack action (not bonus action)
- **Topple**: Target makes Con save or falls prone
- **Push**: Shove target away on hit
- **Sap**: Reduce target's damage dealing
- **Slow**: Reduce target's speed
- **Vex**: Gain advantage on next attack against same target

## Creature Sizes
- Tiny: 2.5x2.5 ft (0.5 tiles)
- Small/Medium: 5x5 ft (1 tile)
- Large: 10x10 ft (2x2 tiles)
- Huge: 15x15 ft (3x3 tiles)
- Gargantuan: 20x20+ ft (4x4+ tiles)

## Mounted Combat
- Mount/dismount costs half your speed
- Controlled mount: Dash, Disengage, Dodge only
- Independent mount: Full agency
- If mount knocked prone: Reaction to dismount and land on feet

## Spellcasting in Combat
- Most spells: 1 action casting time
- Some spells: 1 bonus action, 1 reaction, or longer
- **Concentration**: Some spells require ongoing concentration
  - Taking damage: Con save (DC 10 or half damage, whichever higher) to maintain
  - Only 1 concentration spell at a time
  - Broken by: incapacitation, death, another concentration spell
- **2024 RULE**: Only 1 leveled spell per turn (cantrips unlimited)
- **Spell Attack**: d20 + spellcasting ability mod + proficiency
- **Spell Save DC**: 8 + spellcasting ability mod + proficiency
